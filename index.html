⚡ Did You Know?

Biomedical engineers design pacemakers — tiny devices that help regulate heart rhythm using electrical impulses. Over 3 million people worldwide rely on them!

Heart rate monitors use electrocardiography (ECG/EKG) to detect electrical signals from your heart. Biomedical engineers have developed wearable versions that can detect irregular rhythms, predict heart attacks, and even send alerts to doctors remotely. Modern smartwatches use photoplethysmography (PPG) — shining light through your skin to measure blood flow!
